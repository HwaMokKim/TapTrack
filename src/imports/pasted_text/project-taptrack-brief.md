DESIGN BRIEF 2.0: "Project TapTrack" — The Behavioral Guardian

To: UI/UX Designer

Core Mission: Move from Reporting (the past) to Guiding (the future). The UI must answer the user's subconscious question: "Can I afford this right now without hurting my future self?"

1. The "Golden Screen": The Safe-to-Spend Dashboard

Instead of showing a "Total Spent" (which causes anxiety), the main view focuses on Availability.





The Hero Metric: A large, elegant "Safe-to-Spend Today" or "Remaining for the Week" number.



Cognitive Load Reduction: Use a "Fuel Gauge" visual rather than a complex list. If the gauge is full, the user feels peace. If it's low, the Puppy looks "thoughtful" (not sad).



Standout Feature (The 'Monarch' influence): A "Recurring" progress bar that shows how much of their "Safe" money is already spoken for by subscriptions (Netflix, Gym).

2. Enhanced User Flow: The "Intuitive" Back Tap

State 1: The Predictive Overlay





AI Pre-fill: Using GPS and Time, the app doesn't just open a Numpad; it opens with a suggested label.





Example: If at a Grocery Store, the button says "Log Groceries".



One-Handed Ergonomics: The Numpad is positioned at the bottom 40% of the screen for easy thumb reach.

State 2: Emotional Integration (The "Value" Check)





The "Vibe" Toggle: Next to the "Save" button, include three small, minimalist icons: 🙂 | 😐 | ☹️.



The Psychology: This forces the user to log how they felt about the purchase.





Result: At the end of the month, the app shows: "You spent $200 on things that made you feel ☹️. Let's cut those out next month!" This is the Amazon-level personalization—it teaches the user about their own happiness.

3. The "Puppy" Evolution: From Mascot to Coach

To avoid "Juvenile Fatigue," the puppy must be a functional part of the UI.





The Reward Store: Users earn "Kibble" (points) for every consecutive day they log. They can use Kibble to unlock minimalist "Skins" for the app (e.g., Dark Mode Gold, Sage Green Theme).



The Intervention: If the user logs an expensive item, the Puppy appears with a small speech bubble: "This is 20% of our 'New Shoes' goal. Still want to proceed?" (Choice, not Judgment).

4. The "Missing Link" Features (To Stand Out)

A. The "Ghost" Entry (Handling Forgetfulness)





If a user hasn't logged in 24 hours, the Puppy sends a "Quiet" notification: "Hey, I noticed a gap in our map. Want to log a quick 'General' expense to keep our streak?"

B. The "Split-Bill" Slide





On the Numpad, add a "÷" button. If they spent $100 but are getting $50 back, they hit "100 ÷ 2" and the app only tracks $50. This solves the #1 pain point of manual tracking.

5. Updated Visual Style: "Quiet Luxury"





Typography: Use Tabular Sans for numbers. This ensures that as the numbers change, they don't "jump" or wiggle on the screen (Standard in high-end Swiss banking apps).



Haptics: Every tap on the Numpad should have a "Taptic" click. The "Save" button should have a "Heartbeat" haptic. This makes the digital app feel like a physical, high-quality tool.

Summary of Improved Logic for the Designer:





Primary View: "Safe-to-Spend" (Forward-looking).



Input: Back-Tap -> Numpad + Emotional Emoji -> Save (Under 2 seconds).



Reinforcement: Puppy "High-Five" + Kibble Points for the Reward Store.



No Shame: Muted grays for overspending; focus on "Adjusting for Tomorrow."
